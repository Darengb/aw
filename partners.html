Model

A work-first model built around measurable outcomes.

Our approach is grounded in the belief that meaningful employment is the most effective path out of poverty. We design programs that prioritize rapid attachment to work, backed by training, wraparound services, and ongoing support to sustain employment.

01

Work readiness

We build both hard and soft skills that employers need—communication, customer service, computer literacy, workplace expectations and more—so participants are prepared to succeed, not just to interview.

02

Training & education

We connect participants to certificate programs, ESL, high school equivalency, and other training aligned with local labor market demand, so every hour invested supports a viable career goal.

03

Employment & placement

As soon as participants are ready, we match their goals and work history to open opportunities using our employer network. We fast-track them to interviews and help agencies meet placement targets.

04

Support & advancement

Once participants enter employment, we provide transition support, problem-solving, and additional training and certifications, helping them retain their jobs, increase their earnings, and reduce returns to public assistance.

Examples

Program areas and example partnerships.

Our work spans multiple funding streams and program types. A few examples:

SNAP Employment & Training (SNAP E&T)

Example: Multi-million dollar, hybrid performance-based contracts delivering Career Advance, Career Compass, and Youth Pathways services across New York City boroughs.

TANF work programs & family economic security

Example: Providers under performance-based TANF contracts in jurisdictions like Washington, DC, focused on participation and employment within a broader two-generation strategy.

WIOA and related workforce initiatives

Example: Operation of job search assistance and "back to work" programs compared in rigorous evaluations such as the NYC Job Search Assistance study.

Disability employment programs (Ticket to Work)

Example: America Works of New York registered as a national Employment Network under SSA's Ticket to Work program, providing employment supports to SSI/SSDI beneficiaries.

Reentry and justice-involved populations

Example: Integration of employment services with corrections and reentry initiatives supported by U.S. Department of Labor and other funders.

Process

How we build and run programs with you.

We know every jurisdiction is different. Our implementation approach is collaborative and structured, so you get a partner—not just a vendor.

01

Co-design & planning

We start by understanding your policy goals, funding streams, and performance requirements. Together, we define target populations, service flows, and outcome metrics that align with your strategy and federal guidelines.

02

Rapid stand-up of operations

We recruit and train staff, secure and equip office locations where needed, and connect with local employer and community networks. Our history includes standing up new locations within about a month of contract award when necessary.

03

Service delivery & continuous improvement

We deliver the full blend of services—outreach, assessment, work readiness, training, placement, and retention—while using data to monitor progress and continuously refine strategies.

04

Reporting, evaluation & scaling

We provide regular performance reports and collaborate on evaluations to support accountability and inform future policy. Successful pilots can be scaled to additional sites or populations.
